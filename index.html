About Me

I believe that the beauty of mathematics and the power of technology can transform how we learn.

As a Mathematics student at Columbia University with a 4.09 GPA, I've dedicated myself to bridging the gap between complex theoretical concepts and accessible learning experiences. My work at the intersection of mathematics, computer science, and education technology reflects my core belief: everyone deserves the tools to understand the logic that shapes our world.

Through my research at Columbia's Department of Computer Science, I've developed interactive systems that gamify formal logic learning, making abstract concepts tangible and engaging. Whether it's translating natural language into first-order logic or creating grid-based learning games, my goal remains the same: making learning accessible through technology.

Research Experience

Columbia Center for Teaching and Learning

Columbia University - Department of Computer Science Student Researcher October 2024 - Present

Project: Logic Learner - Interactive First-Order Logic Education

The Challenge: Learning formal logic is often daunting for students. Traditional methods fail to engage learners with the abstract nature of logical reasoning.

  • Built a web-based platform for interactive logic learning using JavaScript
  • Developed a bidirectional translation system between natural language and First-Order Logic
  • Created an automated logical expression evaluator
  • Gamified the learning process to increase student engagement

The Impact: These tools make formal reasoning accessible to students who might otherwise struggle with abstract mathematical concepts, democratizing access to rigorous logical thinking.
